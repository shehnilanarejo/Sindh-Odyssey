Sukkur Sindh, Pakistan



Explore Sukkur, a historic city along the banks of the Indus River in Sindh, Pakistan. Known for its cultural richness and historical significance, Sukkur is home to the iconic Sukkur Barrage, a marvel of engineering that regulates the flow of the Indus River and supports agriculture in the region. Discover its bustling markets, ancient temples, and mosques, and experience the blend of tradition and modernity that defines this vibrant city.Sukkur is approximately 400 kilometers northeast of Karachi, accessible via the National Highway N-5. The journey by road takes about 5 to 6 hours, offering scenic views of rural Sindh along the way


Places to visit in Sukkur


Sadhu Belo


Sadh Belo also spelt as Sadh Bela, or Sat, is an island in the Indus River near Sukkur, Sindh, Pakistan that is famous for its highly revered Hindu temples.[2] The temples are associated with the syncretic Udasi movement of Sikhism.[3] The island is famous for Teerath Asthan which is the biggest Hindu temple in Pakistan. The complex has eight other temples, a library, dining areas, a huge garden, along with rooms and residences for monks and people who want to stay on the island on a spiritual retreat


Minaret of Masum Shah

The Mausam Shah Minar in Sukkur, Pakistan, is an elegant historical monument commemorating the legacy of Mausam Shah, a revered local figure. Constructed in the 19th century, this tall and intricately designed minaret stands as a testament to the architectural style of its era. The structure, adorned with detailed carvings and inscriptions, reflects the rich cultural heritage of Sukkur. Situated in a prominent location, it serves as a historical landmark and a symbol of the region's artistic and architectural achievements. The Mausam Shah Minar is not only a site of historical interest but also a popular spot for visitors exploring Sukkur's cultural landscape.


Sukkur Barrage



Sukkur Barrage is a barrage on the River Indus near the city of Sukkur in the Sindh province of Pakistan. The barrage was built during the British Raj from 1923 to 1932 and was named Lloyd Barrage. The Sukkur Barrage, is the pride of Pakistan's irrigation system as it is the largest single irrigation network of its kind in the world. It irrigates from Sukkur district in the north, to Mirpurkhas/Tharparkar and Hyderabad districts in the south of Sindh, almost all parts of the province
