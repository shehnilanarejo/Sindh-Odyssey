Tharparkar Sindh, Pakistan


Discover the mesmerizing landscapes, ancient rocky hills, and mystical deserts of Tharparkar—a destination that promises an unforgettable experience. This district, with a history spanning 2,500 years and symbolizing peace and harmony, offers breathtaking views of the Karoonjhar mountains, vibrant villages, and a rich tapestry of historical sites and cultural heritage.Located merely a 3-hour drive from Hyderabad or a scenic 5-hour journey from Karachi, Tharparkar unveils charming surprises and hidden beauties, ensuring a journey filled with unique encounters and unforgettable memories.



Places to visit in Tharparkar



Gori Temple



The Gori Temple is also known as Gori Mandar, a Jain Temple in Nagarparkar. It’s located 90km from Mithi. It was built in 1375-1376 CE. The temple was exclusively allocated to the 23rd Jain Tirthankar Lord Parshwanath. This temple along with other Jain temples of Nagarparkar were inscribed on the tentative list for UNESCO World Heritage status in 2016 as the Nagarparkar Cultural Landscape.


Naukot Fort

Naukot fort is situated about 5km from the Naukot town, which lies on edge of the desert in District Thar. The fort is said to have been constructed by Mir Karam Ali Khan Talpur in 1814 A.D. it is roughly square in plan measuring 640×600 feet. The fortification wall of the fort which gives a tapered look has been built of baked bricks laid in mud mortar. On the southeast corner, there exists an arched gateway flanked by semi-circular bastions hiding the main entrance. The fort is unique in the area and attracts a large number of visitors from across the country and abroad.


Ghadi Bhit

Ghadi Bhit is an elevation point of the town built on a sandhill and gives a breathtaking panoramic view of the Mithi town.
There was also a check post of the Talpur rulers that was demolished over time. This check post was created to monitor external invaders and bandits from Gujarat and Rajasthan at one time.
As the sun sets in the rear of the hill, the city lights shine so it feels like someone has lit candles across the town. While the stars in the sky make this scene even more attractive.
Ghadi Bhit is a popular tourist spot and a must-visit place if you are coming to Tharparkar.
